Ireland Tours

T

he Emerald Isle, as it has long been known, has always had a strong sense of identity. How has the culture of an island of only 6.5 million people spread around the globe and become a distictive part of life worldwide? As many as 80 million people can trace their roots back to Ireland. Ireland, perhaps more than anywhere else, is an experience — a place separated, in a way, not just from Europe, but from all of reality. From the history of the Troubles in Northern Ireland to the cultural isolation of the Irish-speaking Gaeltacht, from the famous Cliffs of Moher in the west to the thriving metropolis of Dublin in the east, from the volcanic Giant's Causeway in the north to the glacial Burren in the west, Ireland, somehow, never changes. Ireland is a pinnacle of life in the harsh waves of the Atlantic — despite all odds, it will remain standing forever.
