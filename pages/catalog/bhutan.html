Bhutan Tours

B

hutan is the last independent Buddhist kingdom anywhere in the world. With a population of approximately 750,000 people, Bhutan's serene perch among the Himalayas is unrivaled around the globe. Bhutan is unique in other ways, too: despite being classified as one of the United Nations' "Least Developed Countries", Bhutan has been consistently ranked as one of the happiest countries on Earth; Bhutan transitioned from an absolute monarchy to a democracy only in 2008, courtesy of a king who recognized democracy's importance despite resistance from the people; within Bhutan lies the tallest unclimbed mountain in the world, never summitted because of religious beliefs. Also in Bhutan is one of the world's most dangerous airports, with only eight living pilots qualified to land there. Bhutan, too, is a place where "Gross National Happiness" is more valuable than GDP; where tourism was discouraged to prevent harm it might wreak upon Bhutan's already-fragile culture. Where, truly, across the globe, can one find somewhere like Bhutan? The only carbon-negative country in the world, and yet one of only four widely-recognized sovereign nations with no diplomatic ties to the United States? Bhutan is, and will forever be, extraordinary.
